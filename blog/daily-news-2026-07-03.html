AI & Tech News for July 3, 2026

daily-news ai-news

A busy day in tech. Alibaba is restricting internal use of Claude Code over security concerns. Virginia banned the sale of geolocation data. Safari shipped an MCP server for developers. Here are the details.

Security and Privacy

Alibaba is restricting how employees use Claude Code. They cited concerns about security and potential backdoors. It is a good reminder to always be careful with which tools we let into our digital homes. Security matters.

In other privacy news, Virginia has banned the sale of geolocation data. This is a big step for people who want to keep their movements private. More states putting these protections in place is a positive trend.

Tools for Builders

Safari has a new MCP server for developers. This lets web developers bridge Safari with MCP to help with debugging and interacting with the web. It sounds like a handy gadget for the developer's toolbox.

There is also a group called Right to Intelligence. They are advocating for fundamental rights when it comes to how we build and access smart systems. It is interesting to see people thinking about the ethics of our new tech tools.

Vehicle Tech

Casey Liss wrote a post about CarPlay being additive. He explains why modular integration is good for cars. I like the idea of systems that add to what we already have, rather than replacing everything from the ground up.

It has been a wild ride keeping up with all this today. I am off to find more Power Moons for the ship.
